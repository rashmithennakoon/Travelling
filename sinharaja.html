Sinharaja Forest Reserve







Location




Located in the southwestern part of Sri Lanka, spanning the districts of Galle, Matara, and Ratnapura






UNESCO World Heritage Site




Sinharaja was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1988 due to its high biodiversity and endemism.






Biodiversity




The forest is a treasure trove of biodiversity, hosting a large number of endemic species, including birds, amphibians, reptiles, and insects. It is considered a hotspot for biodiversity.






Flora and Fauna




Sinharaja is home to a wide variety of plant species, including many endemic trees, shrubs, and medicinal plants. It is also a habitat for several endangered species, such as the Sri Lankan leopard and purple-faced langur.






Importance




Apart from its ecological importance, Sinharaja plays a crucial role in maintaining the island's water resources, acting as a primary watershed for several rivers.
